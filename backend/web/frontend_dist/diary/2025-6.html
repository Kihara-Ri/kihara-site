晚上捡起来那个搁置了很久的问题——你如何看待AI时代的学习？生成式AI会对人们的学习方式造成怎样的改变？你认为“AI无法解决你所不知道自身不知道”这个观点正确吗？
我觉得这是这个时代最重要的一个议题，唯有搞清楚这个，才能明白我们作为人类，生活在新时代的作用和意义。其中有一个非常经典的乔哈里窗(Johari Window)，即未知的未知(unknown unknowns)，考虑到以后可能会经常被问到这个问题，甚至是考试和面试，因此在下面我将使用英语来给出我的理解和回答
---
From my point of view, AI is indeed a powerful learning companion — in many cases, even better than traditional tutorials. When you're studying, AI is always available, offering infinite knowledge and endless patience. This makes it essential for users to develop the skill of asking good questions — because effective learning in the age of AI depends largely on the quality of the questions we ask.
However, there are also important limitations to keep in mind. One of the most significant is that AI cannot answer what you don't know you don't know. In other words, if you're completely unaware of a concept, like Fermat's Last Theorem, you would never think to ask about it in the first place. This presents a challenge when entering unfamiliar fields — simply asking AI questions might not be the most effective way to build a broad and accurate understanding.
In fact, AI may sometimes lead you too deep into unnecessary details. When you're missing foundational knowledge, AI might provide sophisticated explanations or solutions that are more confusing than helpful. This often happened to me when I was writing code with limited experience — the more I followed AI's suggestions, the further I strayed from the right approach. Sometimes the original problem was not that complex — a different perspective or a simpler method would have solved it more effectively.
In reality, those with greater knowledge tend to benefit the most from AI. Some people are able to develop a full application with AI in just a few days or use it to explore deep mathematical problems. Meanwhile, others might get little to no value from it simply because they don’t know where to begin. This shows that AI enhances the abilities of those who are already equipped to learn.
To keep pace with rapidly advancing technology, I believe we should adopt the following habits:
- Always formulate new questions and actively engage AI in dialogue to explore them.
- Seek to understand the underlying principles behind theories and methods, not just the surface-level answers.
- Read widely and deeply to gain a broader perspective and avoid falling into the trap of local optima.
- Avoid over-relying on AI for tasks like coding, especially before developing a solid foundational understanding.
In summary, AI is a powerful tool — but it's still just that: a tool. Its effectiveness depends entirely on how we choose to use it. The more we grow our own knowledge and critical thinking, the more value AI can bring into our learning journeys.

这两天突然对一些概念有了新的理解，比如之前看到的(隐)马尔可夫什么什么、切比雪夫什么什么、傅立叶变换等等，这些只要没接触过就觉得非常高大上很复杂的东西，其实只是一种比较专有的思想或者处理方法，提出这些方法的人可能只是有了一个很基本的想法，然后后人在他们的基础上对这个思想进行开发，然后应用到各个领域，然后由于领域之间亦有不同，因此多出了很多某某方法之类的东西，其实只要抓住了核心本质便不难理解，比如傅立叶变换，其本人并没有推导出傅立叶变换，而只是使用了傅立叶级数，他认为所有的函数都可以用三角函数的无穷级数来表达，称为傅立叶级数，毕竟是他自己提出来的，只不过后来的数学家在他的基础上，推广出了傅立叶变换，前者用于周期函数，而后者则没有这个限制，非周期函数，无限区间上的函数都可以应用傅立叶变换
